## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?**

**LLM assessment — YES, with specific tells:**

The site passes a quick scroll test but fails the design-literate second look. Two absolute bans fire on every page section: **Numbered section markers as default scaffolding** (01 / 02 / 03 / 04 eyebrows) and **Tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section heading** ("About", "Toolset", "Selected Work", "Contact"). Both appear simultaneously on every section. This is the single most reliable AI portfolio tell in 2026 — the `[number] ─────── [label]` row has appeared on the overwhelming majority of AI-generated developer portfolios, and design-literate recruiters pattern-match it in under a second.

The font choice (Syne display + Manrope body + JetBrains Mono everywhere) is technically functional but Syne has become the default "modern portfolio display font" in AI tooling, and JetBrains Mono is applied as lazy technical costume — it appears in nav links, tags, stats, section labels, buttons, footer, form labels. A monospace font used on every short label is signalling "I am a developer" through repetition rather than earning that read through constraint.

The hero stats block (5+ Projects, 3 Years Building, 7+ Tech Mastered) hits the hero-metric template anti-pattern. These three number+label pairs below the headline are a SaaS cliché transplanted into a portfolio.

The emerald #10b981 accent on near-black #0a0a0a is "terminal hacker green" — the first-order color reflex for developer portfolios. The color strategy is Restrained (neutrals + one accent) when the brand register calls for Committed or stronger.

The strongest and most distinctive element is the Projects section floating cursor image — the lerp-smoothed image that follows the mouse is genuinely clever and not a cliché. That interaction keeps the site from failing entirely.

**Deterministic scan:** The automated detector returned 0 findings. No gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no glassmorphism, no identical card grids were detected. Clean on the mechanical checks.

**Visual overlays:** Browser visualization was not used for this run. Fallback to source analysis only.

## Overall Impression

Technically executed, visually legible, but immediately readable as AI-assembled. The Projects hover interaction is the one moment the site feels like it has a point of view. Everything else follows the developer portfolio playbook so closely that a visitor's first impression is template, not person. The biggest single opportunity: remove the numbered eyebrow scaffolding from every section and let the sections earn their own identity — that one change closes the largest gap between "competent AI output" and "a person's portfolio."

## What's Working

**1. The project hover interaction.** The lerp-smoothed floating image preview that follows the cursor is the most distinctive element on the page. It's clever, it works smoothly, and it's not a standard portfolio affordance. The project list layout itself (number, title, role, description, tags, year in a single row) is also clean and scannable.

**2. Color contrast is genuinely solid.** #a3a3a3 muted text on #0a0a0a background is ~8.4:1, well above WCAG AAA. The accent #10b981 on #0a0a0a hits ~8.1:1. Previous sessions fixed what were critical failures (#1A4C39 and #525252); those fixes held.

**3. Mobile menu is properly built.** Scroll lock, staggered link reveals, fullscreen overlay with clean animation, proper aria-expanded, keyboard-dismissible. This is work that's easy to get wrong and this version got it right.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Numbered section eyebrows fire on every section — absolute ban
**What:** Every section opens with `[01] ─────── About`, `[02] ─────── Toolset`, etc. This triggers both the "numbered section markers as scaffolding" and "tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section" absolute bans simultaneously.  
**Why it matters:** This is the highest-signal AI portfolio tell. Design-literate hiring managers and recruiters have pattern-matched this template. It reads as "no one made this" rather than "Tways Navarro made this." The numbers carry no sequential meaning (skills don't come after about because of order; they're just sections), and the labels duplicate what the section content immediately communicates.  
**Fix:** Remove the entire eyebrow row from all sections. Let each section open directly with its headline or content. One considered section divider system (e.g., a single ruled line with no label) or no dividers at all. Reserve numbering for the Projects list (01–05 on projects is legitimate since those are genuinely enumerated items).  
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

### [P1] JetBrains Mono is used as developer costume, not voice
**What:** Mono is applied to nav links, all tag chips, stats labels, section eyebrows, buttons, skill names, form labels, project year, footer — everything that isn't a display heading or body paragraph. This triggers the brand ban: "Monospace as lazy shorthand for 'technical / developer.'"  
**Why it matters:** When mono is applied to 80% of text elements, it stops being a voice marker and starts being a style tic. It reads as "I used the developer font everywhere." The restraint that makes mono meaningful — using it only where the machine/code association is earned (code snippets, technical labels, terminal output) — is lost.  
**Fix:** Reserve JetBrains Mono for 3-4 specific contexts: the logo "TWICECODED", the nav links (legitimate brand system choice), actual code/tag labels in Projects, and the form status messages. Switch all other labels (stats, section counts, skill names, footer copy) to either Manrope light or a tracked uppercase Manrope.  
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable typeset`

### [P2] Hero metric stats: hero-metric template anti-pattern
**What:** Three numbers with small mono labels below the hero headline — "5+ Projects Shipped / 3 Years Building / 7+ Tech Mastered" — is the SaaS hero-metric template.  
**Why it matters:** It looks assembled, not chosen. "3 Years Building" as a stat also undersells a student portfolio and invites skepticism about seniority. "7+ Tech Mastered" is vague and impossible to verify.  
**Fix:** Replace with 1-2 concrete, specific claims that aren't number-metrics. E.g., "Based in Iloilo City, PH — open to remote" as a single line, or a short punchy bio fragment. Or remove entirely and let the CTA buttons be the only next step below the headline.  
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill`

### [P2] Pharmatrack link (#) creates false affordance
**What:** Project 02 (Pharmatrack) has `link: '#'` — but the full hover interaction fires (cursor pointer, underline animation, ArrowUpRight icon). Visitors click expecting to open a repo and hit the top of the page.  
**Why it matters:** Trust erosion. One broken link on a portfolio signals inattention. Recruiters click links.  
**Fix:** Either add a clear visual indicator that the repo is private (e.g., a "Private" label replacing the ArrowUpRight), or add a `title` attribute, or remove the row's link behavior entirely when `link === '#'`.  
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden`

### [P2] No prefers-reduced-motion coverage for CSS animations
**What:** The marquee animation (`globals.css`), the `animate-pulse` badge in Hero, and the scroll indicator animation have no `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` override. Framer Motion handles its own animations automatically, but CSS-only animations don't.  
**Why it matters:** Users who have set reduced motion for vestibular or epilepsy reasons will still get infinitely looping animations. This is a WCAG 2.1 AA requirement.  
**Fix:** Add to `globals.css`:  
```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  .marquee-inner { animation: none; }
  .animate-pulse { animation: none; }
}
```  
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable audit`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Time Recruiter — scanning 30 portfolios today):**  
- Sees "TWICECODED" in the navbar and "Tways Navarro" in the About section. Never learns the relationship. Is TWICECODED a studio? A freelance brand? A personal handle? The identity split creates cognitive friction before the work is seen.  
- Clicks Pharmatrack (interesting name, relevant to healthcare). Goes nowhere. Tries again. Moves on with reduced trust.  
- On mobile, tries to preview projects — the hover image preview is desktop-only. Mobile gets no visual preview at all. The project list on mobile is text-only with no imagery, weaker than the desktop experience.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User — checking portfolios between meetings):**  
- All 5 project Unsplash images (`project.image`) are loaded eagerly in the floating preview div (`PROJECTS.map` renders all 5 `<img>` tags at mount). On mobile these all load even though the floating preview never shows. Wasted bandwidth on a slow connection.  
- The floating project preview (`position: fixed` + computed `rect` from `containerRef`) is calculated from desktop layout values that don't apply at mobile viewport sizes. The preview div itself is mobile-unreachable (mouse-only interaction) but still renders in the DOM and loads all images.  
- "Available for work" badge at the top of Hero — this is the clearest signal for Casey's purpose but requires scrolling past if they open mid-page.

**Hiring Manager (Project-Specific Persona — has a specific role, wants to evaluate technical depth):**  
- Reads "5+ Projects Shipped" and "3 Years Building" and "7+ Tech Mastered." Vague. "Mastered" is a high bar. "7+" doesn't tell them which 7.  
- Clicks through to BoardMate (GitHub repo) — what do they find? A README? Working code? The portfolio doesn't set expectations about what a project link will show.  
- The About copy says "striving Full-Stack developer" — "striving" reads as self-deprecating in a hiring context. It signals honest ambition but undercuts the confidence the design is otherwise trying to project.

## Minor Observations

- The About section `h2` uses `fontSize: 'clamp(3rem, 7vw, 5.5rem)'` which is below the 6rem ceiling — fine. But the Hero uses `clamp(3.5rem, 10.5vw, 10.5rem)` — at max width that's 168px, above the 6rem guideline. At most desktop viewport widths (~1440px) the computed size would be ~151px. This is a deliberate display choice for the portfolio, but it risks feeling oversized at 1600px+ viewports.
- `text-wrap: balance` is not applied to h1 or h2 elements. Adding it would improve line-break distribution especially at mid-range viewport widths.
- The Skills section `opacity-30 cursor-not-allowed` inactive skills look like disabled UI. For a portfolio, "coming soon" or simply omitting inactive skills would be cleaner than showing them grayed out.
- The `role:` field on Project objects ("Full-Stack Developer" for all 5 projects) adds no signal when all projects have the same role label.
- The About section body copy starts "I'm a *striving* Full-Stack developer" — "striving" should be removed. It's honest but reads as lack of confidence in a hire-me context.

## Questions to Consider

- "TWICECODED" appears in nav and footer; "Tways Navarro" appears in About. Which identity are you presenting? Committing to one (the person, or the brand) would sharpen everything.
- If the numbered eyebrow rows were removed from every section, what would hold the sections together? If the answer is "nothing," that reveals the sections need stronger individual identities — each one should earn its own opening.
- The Projects section is by far the most distinctive. What if it were given more space — the hero third of the page rather than the middle section?
- What would this portfolio look like if the primary color were not emerald green? Green + dark = terminal. Is that intentional, or is a different brand statement possible?
