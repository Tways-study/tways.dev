# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences:

- **Recruiters & hiring managers** — scanning for a full-time hire; need fast signal on capability, taste, and reliability. They skim; the work and the confidence of the presentation must convert before they scroll far.
- **Clients & startup founders** — have a project, looking for a freelance developer or designer they can trust. They linger longer; they want to understand how this person thinks, not just what they've shipped.

Both groups arrive skeptical of portfolios by default. The site must feel different from the noise — not through spectacle, but through precision and intentionality.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Tways Navarro — web developer and designer based in Iloilo City, PH. Purpose: earn trust and generate inbound opportunities (freelance, internship, full-time). Success means a visitor finishes the page feeling they'd like to work with this person.

## Brand Personality

Clean · Calm · Dependable

Voice: direct, assured, without arrogance. The copy says "building precise, purposeful digital experiences" — that's the register. Not breathless enthusiasm, not cold technical listing. Someone who cares about craft and wants you to know it without needing to say so twice.

Reference: Linear.app — ultra-clean dark UI, precise typography, confident spacing. The quality is self-evident; it doesn't announce itself.

## Anti-references

- Generic portfolio templates — sites that look like every other developer's site: same hero layout, same card grid, same color gradient. If it could belong to anyone, it belongs to no one.
- Corporate / sterile — cold, impersonal design that could be a SaaS landing page or an agency site. The portfolio belongs to a specific person with a specific sensibility.

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries the hierarchy** — the type system does the heavy lifting. Decoration earns its place; if removing an element doesn't change the reading, remove it.
2. **Quiet confidence** — restraint in motion and color. Animations should feel inevitable, not impressive. The work speaks; the frame stays quiet.
3. **Every element accountable** — no ambient filler. Spatial decisions are intentional. Dense where it should be dense; open where it should breathe.
4. **Human signal in technical work** — personal voice in copy and micro-details creates warmth without ornament. This is a person's site, not a brand's.
5. **Anti-template discipline** — if a layout choice looks like a portfolio template, redesign it. Distinctiveness comes from specificity, not style trends.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Reduced-motion: Framer Motion animations should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Custom cursor is already gated behind `pointer: fine` media query — correct
- Sufficient contrast required for all text against backgrounds, including Brunswick Green on black (currently low contrast — worth auditing)
